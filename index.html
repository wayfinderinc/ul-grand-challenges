During the 20th century, humanity crossed a critical threshold—and the relationship between our species and the planet changed forever.

But our forebears invented increasingly sophisticated technologies to push against those limits.

Now, we live in the Anthropocene: a geological epoch in which people have become not only the planet's defining force, but also the principal architects of its future.

Our Commitment

The Grand Challenges

Resilience for a Sustainable Future

In recent years, renewable energy technologies have emerged as one of the first and highest priority solutions to climate change.

Solar panels and storage batteries have already begun to transform the power grid and our transportation systems, and a host of new technologies are poised to reduce the world's carbon emissions even further.

Yet we must also attend to the risks these technologies present. The Key chemicals inside an current electric vehicle's batteries, for example, are highly flammable, and pose life-threatening harm if they aren't manufactured, stored, and recycled correctly.

That's where UL Research Institutes comes in. For years, our Electrochemical Safety Research Institute (ESRI) has played a critical role in mitigating the risks associated with renewable energy products. ESRI continues to push existing renewable technologies beyond their current limits.

The Grand Challenges

Individual and Societal Health in the 21st Century

From the plastics in our medical devices to the fertilizers that grow our crops, synthetic chemicals are ubiquitous — and even more so than we once thought.

To take just one example, recent studies have detected microplastic polymers in the deepest valleys of the ocean, the highest peaks of the Himalayas, and throughout the human body.

While history is replete with cautionary tales of chemical exposure — from leaded gas to DDT — many of the synthetic compounds that suffuse our world are poorly understood. In some cases, their effects are subtle, and can take years or decades to measure.

That is why UL Research Institutes is shedding new light on the health impacts of manufactured products and chemicals.

At present, our Chemical Insights Research Institute places special emphasis on e-cigarette emissions, flame retardants, 3-D printer emissions, and air pollution—all of which have global reach.

As we learn more about these and other chemicals of concern, we will share our findings with governments, businesses, and other stakeholders to encourage the development of safer products and policies.

The Grand Challenges

Safety at the Human-Digital Interface

In recent years, it has become clear that digital intelligence will unleash a new technological revolution—and indeed, that revolution has already begun.

However, the nature of that progress — who it helps, and who it might hurt — will depend on what we do now to address unintended consequences.

Some of the risks of digital intelligence are obvious, like a faulty algorithm that causes modern disasters, while others are more subtle.

Many scholars have pointed out that digital intelligence requires us to write human values into code; if we fail to root out the biases in our technologies, they will reproduce themselves at scale, and undermine the more prosperous and equitable world we seek to build.

UL Research Institutes is convening experts across sectors, disciplines, and geographies to define the 21st century are designed with safety, security, and equity in mind—and advance those values around the world.
